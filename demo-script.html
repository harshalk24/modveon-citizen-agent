This is the next layer we're accelerating, and the one that completes our Verified Operating System. Here's where we're headed.

Intro

Plays before the first scenario
Five real government situations. And in every single one — the agent has already done the work before the person even opens the app.

Scenario 1 — Newborn Registration

Sarah Williams · 3 days after birth
Sarah just had a baby. Three days ago. And right now, there are two government deadlines quietly ticking — a 42-day window to register the birth, and a separate one for health insurance. She has no idea either exists.

The agent reaches out first. It pulls her Social Insurance record, the hospital discharge certificate, her employer health plan — pre-fills the entire registration form on its own. The only thing it can't find? The baby's name.

One name. That's it. Birth registered. Health insurance enrolled. WhatsApp sent — before she's even left the hospital. Two office visits that simply never happened.

Scenario 2 — Vehicle Renewal

Alex Morgan · licence expires in 11 days
Alex has no idea his vehicle licence expires in 11 days. And he definitely doesn't know about two unpaid parking tickets from March — the kind TCD holds against counter renewals, even when the online portal looks completely fine.

The agent caught both. Built a combined payment before Alex even opened the app. One tap to confirm. Tickets cleared, licence renewed through June 2027. What used to mean a trip to TCD with a folder of receipts — gone. Done in one tap.

Scenario 3 — Social Insurance

Camille Outerbridge · employer gap since March
Camille never filed a complaint. The agent found this on its own, while reviewing something else — and that almost never happens in government.

Her employer stopped contributing after her March promotion. Her voluntary contributions lapsed 14 months ago. Two separate problems, quietly draining her pension forecast — and she had no idea about either.

Then it finds something remarkable. An open case from April, same employer, zero follow-up. No new filing needed — it attaches her records directly. Four days later: resolved. Contributions backdated, credited in full. Two issues, both closed. Not a single call. Not a single office visit.

Scenario 4 — Work Permit Renewal

Daniel Chen · permit expires in 58 days
Daniel's permit expires in 58 days. The agent has already run the checks, verified compliance, pre-filled everything. One tap — submitted.

Six checks in seconds. And right there in the results, something Daniel would never have looked for himself: nineteen years and eleven months of residency. One month from Permanent Residency. Reminder already set for February — everything ready the moment he qualifies.

Two days later: approved. What used to mean a trip to Hamilton and weeks of waiting — gone.

Scenario 5 — Business Registration

Kezia Trott · registering Bermuda Coastal Catering
Kezia wants to register her catering business. One tap — and watch what happens.

The agent checks her growth plan, compares structures, then finds something most people miss entirely — an SME grant that closes the moment you incorporate. Most founders register first, ask about grants second, and find out too late. The agent does it in exactly the right order.

Grant applied. Company incorporated. Three agencies registered. Right structure, first try. Kezia got something most founders only wish they'd known about — before she even signed anything.
